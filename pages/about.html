About Me

Hey there. I’m a cybersecurity professional and lifelong Linux enthusiast based in the tri-state area of Iowa, Illinois, and Wisconsin. With over a decade as a Windows System Administrator and two decades of hands-on Linux experience, I specialize in making complex tech understandable—whether it’s for compliance, automation, or just plain curiosity.

My current focus is PCI DSS compliance and secure infrastructure, but my interests run deep: from Python, PowerShell, Bash, and Batch scripting to modding game consoles, reverse engineering binaries, and exploring the weird and wonderful world of cybersecurity.

When I'm not auditing Active Directory or soldering a mod chip into a Nintendo Switch Lite, I’m usually tinkering with automation scripts, building JBOD/ZFS storage setups, or experimenting with weird browser UIs. I’m also a big fan of the small web, minimalist interfaces, and creative problem-solving.

My mission? Demystify technology. I believe the best tools are the ones people feel empowered to use—so I try to break things down into bite-sized, actionable pieces anyone can understand and use.

I’m also the mind behind a few side projects like defaultcreds.lol, a small web aggregator for cybersecurity headlines and tools.

If you’re into hacking, scripting, electronics, 3D printing, old-school emulation, or just need help figuring out why your firewall hates you—stick around. I probably have a script for that.

Want to connect? Shoot me a message. I’m always up for swapping ideas or helping someone figure out their next tech move.
